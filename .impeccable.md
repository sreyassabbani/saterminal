## Design Context

### Users

The primary user is a terminal-comfortable SAT learner who studies frequently and expects the tool to stay out of the way. The core job is focused, repeatable question practice with useful review and progress feedback, entirely from local data.

### Brand Personality

Focused, exact, and calm. The interface should feel authored and dependable: high-taste without decoration, technical without becoming cryptic, and encouraging without becoming playful or noisy.

### Aesthetic Direction

A refined utilitarian TUI with dense-but-breathable information layout. Structure comes from alignment, spacing, restrained separators, and semantic color. Avoid generic dashboard patterns, ornamental chrome, arbitrary truncation, and layouts that expose framework mechanics.

### Design Principles

1. Preserve concentration: show only information useful to the learner's current decision.
2. Make hierarchy spatial: group related controls tightly and separate distinct tasks clearly.
3. Prefer complete readable text over truncation; adapt the composition when space narrows.
4. Use color semantically for focus, correctness, timing, and metadata—not as decoration.
5. Keep every shortcut discoverable on the screen where it is valid.
